Either I find a way, or I create one.

I started programming at twelve, mostly to make the computer do things I was too impatient to do myself. The computer had opinions. We argued. I’ve been arguing happily ever since.* *My first program had one job. It crashed with great enthusiasm.

These days I’m an apprentice software developer at BSI in Zürich. Before that I spent two years at the University of Zurich — long enough to learn a lot, and to notice that I learn fastest with my hands on real code.

When I’m not at a keyboard I’m usually upside down. Gymnastics and athletics have been part of my life for over a decade, and for most of it I’ve also been coaching kids at the local club.* *Coaching kids is mostly debugging, except the bugs run away.

Whatever curiosity is left over goes to self-improvement, biographies, design, psychology, and the occasional unsolvable math problem. I don’t solve them. We just spend time together.

This is my front page: what I’m building now, where I’ve worked, and what I’ve written. Thanks for reading — the margins have jokes.

— Jannis
