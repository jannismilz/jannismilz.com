Either I find a way, or I create one.

I started programming at twelve, mostly to make the computer do things I was too impatient to do myself. The computer had opinions. We argued. I’ve been arguing happily ever since.

These days I’m an apprentice software developer at BSI in Zürich. Before that I spent two years at the University of Zurich — long enough to learn a lot, and to notice that I learn fastest with my hands on real code.

When I’m not at a keyboard I’m usually upside down. Gymnastics and athletics have been part of my life for over a decade, and for most of it I’ve also been coaching kids at the local club.* *Coaching kids is mostly debugging, except the bugs run away.

— Jannis
